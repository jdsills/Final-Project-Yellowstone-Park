Locations

Observe Tower Fall

The 132-foot (40-m) drop of Tower Creek, framed by eroded volcanic pinnacles. Its idyllic setting has inspired many artists, one such artist being Thomas Moran, his painting of the fall played a roll in the establishment of the park in 1872.

Lower Geyser Basin

The Lower Geyser Basin encompasses nearly 12 square miles, with most of the thermal features widely scattered in small groups. Some of the groups include the Fountain Group, Firehole Lake Group, White Dome Group, Great Fountain - White Creek Group, and the Imperial Group.

Mud Volcano

The Mud Volcano is a stinky caldera filled with a pungent rotten egg smell caused by the hydrogen sulfide gas. The location hosts the most acidic features in the park (the Sulpher Caldron)
